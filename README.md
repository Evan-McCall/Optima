# Optima

**An experiment intelligence layer for AI research teams.** Optima connects published research, internal history, and prior results to reduce redundant experimentation and speed up iteration.

## Value Proposition

Help research teams make the most of their experiments to shorten iteration cycles. Time from hypothesis to result is shortened, and wasted compute is minimized.

Put plainly: **we help AI research teams spend less compute on bad experiments and more time on the experiments that actually move the work forward.**

## The Solution

A system that helps AI research teams choose better experiments, reuse prior results efficiently, and shorten the time from idea to validated conclusion — reducing exploratory trial-and-error.

Agents do the legwork:

- **Search recent research papers** for similar hypotheses.
- **Gather context** — previous reports, company context and history, past experiments, and more.

This surfaces what's already known (externally and internally) so teams design targeted, high-signal experiments instead of rediscovering dead ends.

## Example Use Case

A legal startup is building a tool where users upload a long vendor contract and query whether it violates any clauses based on previous vendor agreements. The AI team must build a RAG pipeline, robust evaluations to prevent hallucinations, and ensure security.

They're struggling to decide how to properly evaluate for hallucinations so they can reduce them. They have a few previous experiments and want to find research papers to set up targeted follow-up experiments while keeping the compute budget lean.

They query:

> "Help me set up my next lean experiment for the vendor contract tool that evaluates for hallucinations."

Optima pulls relevant papers, references their prior experiments and internal context, and proposes a focused next experiment — minimizing wasted compute and shortening the path to a validated conclusion.
